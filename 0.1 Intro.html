Introduction

Welcome to Project True-Kin — a Vaults of Vaarn campaign. If you’re reading this, you’ve likely been invited because I think you’d be a good fit at the table.

What This Is

Vaults of Vaarn is a science-fantasy tabletop RPG created by Leo Hunt. It’s set in a far-future, dying-earth world of blue deserts, lost technologies, and half-remembered civilizations.

Project True-Kin is my personal hack of the system, and this site exists as a shared reference for me and my players.

If you want a fast, official overview of the setting, read the one-page intro: What Is Vaarn?

What Vaarn Feels Like

Vaarn is a place of swords, techno-mysticism, and forgotten futures layered atop forgotten futures. Civilizations rise from ruins they barely understand.

While horror is not the campaign’s primary focus, you should expect:

  • Occasional body horror or psychological horror themes
  • An overall content tone roughly equivalent to an R-rated film
  • Meanwhile any X-rated stuff will merely be alluded to rather than described or acted out in full detail.

How This Campaign Plays

  • This is an OSR-style game.
    That means problem-solving, player ingenuity, and meaningful risk matter more than character builds or balance.
  • Characters are fragile.
    Combat is dangerous, especially at low levels. Permanent injury and death are possible. Seeking non-violent solutions is often wise.
  • Death isn’t always the end.
    Characters can be retired voluntarily and may return as NPCs.
  • The rules are living.
    This rules system is barebones and experimental. There are gaps. I will make on-the-spot rulings when needed and strive for consistency. Any explicit rule changes or updates will be communicated clearly before they matter in play.

Table Expectations

  • Inviting new players requires my approval.
  • 4/20- and alcohol-friendly, provided everyone remains attentive and respectful, and obeys the rules of the host and venue. If substance-related behaviour becomes disruptive, it must stop.
  • No driving to or from sessions under the influence.
  • If you have any personal boundaries or social concerns, let me know. We’ll do our best to accommodate and may even be able to update this list.
