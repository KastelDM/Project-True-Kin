Character Creation

1. Print a Character Sheet and Read the Intro

  1. To get started, download and print a Blank Character Sheet. For your reference, here's an example of a filled one.
  2. Read the 1-page setting intro, "What is Vaarn?", then return to these instructions.

2. Roll your Abilities

You have six core Abilities:

  • Strength (STR): melee attacks, readied items, physical power, athletics.
  • Dexterity (DEX): ranged attacks, speed, poise, finesse, acrobatics.
  • Constitution (CON): health recovery, carrying capacity, resistance to elements and intoxicants.
  • Psyche (PSY): ammo conservation, perception, judgement, intuition.
  • Intellect (INT): searching, general and specialized knowledge, language and tech skills (informed by Ancestry and Background).
  • Ego (EGO): morale, willpower, persuasion, performance, charm, deception.

To determine your Ability values, roll 3d6 (three six-sided dice) for each Ability, in order. The lowest result of the three dice on each roll is the Ability's value.

  • Example: You roll a 2, 2, and 6 for Strength. The lowest die is a 2, so your PC's Strength has a value of 2. Repeat this process for the rest of the abilities and record them on your Character Sheet.

3. Determine your Background

Use the generator below to roll a personality trait and background for your character and record them on your character sheet. Your background is meant to be a loose, but important starting point to help inform your character's history and perspective as you roleplay and explore within Vaarn. it will color how non-player characters might react to you, as well as you to them. It is up to you how and to what extent to interpret the results and significance of this roll, but smart roleplay involves drawing on your background to gain the upper hand in applicable situations, and playing to your weaknesses can also enhance your experience.

4. Choose your Ancestry and Give your Character a Name

Choose an Ancestry from the table below. Your ancestry defines what type of life-form you are and what languages you know. The table has a link for each Ancestry where rolling tables may be found and used to inspire your character’s name and appearance. Some of these tables have sparks for demeanour and the like, but the background generator we used in the last step makes them redundant. Only use them if you want your character to be more complex. Finally, each Ancestry also has one or two “Special” features. Be sure to add them to your Features list!

Ancestry Languages known Special
True-Kin (human) Vaarnish, Hegemonic Standard Pure of Blood - You gain Advantage on reaction rolls when you encounter other true-kin. You lose this bonus if you ever become visibly mutated.
Inheritor - You have advantage on checks to identify and use pre-collapse technology.
Cacogen (mutant) Vaarnish Corrupted Blood - You must roll for 1-3 mutations during character creation.
Synth (robot or android) Vaarnish, LogLang syntax Synthetic Flesh - You do not need to eat or breathe and are immune to biological hazards, but you suffer double damage from electrical attacks.
Synthetic Mind - You are vulnerable to hacking and malicious LogLang code.
Newbeast (sapient animal) Vaarnish Beasthood - You gain advantage on checks wherever it would make sense for your animal nature to provide it, and disadvantage in circumstances where your animal nature would be unhelpful.
Mycomorph (corpse-fungus hybrid) Vaarnish Twice Born - You are formed from fungus and the corpse of a human. You can attempt to recall information that your original body knew. This may include information that has otherwise been lost during the Great Collapse.
Detritivor - You can consume organic matter in any state of decay and gain nourishment from it. Your healing from Short Rests is doubled if the meal you eat is rotting.

6. Choose a Mystic Gift or Cybernetic Implant

Pick 1 Mystic Gift or 1 Cybernetic Implant to add to your Features list.

  • If you chose a Cybernetic Implant, be sure to update any relevant Ability values, and keep in mind that you may only ever have 1 Cybernetic implant installed per Ability. Each Ability has a ballot box next to it that you can shade in to designate an installed implant.

6. Add items to your Burdens

You will notice that your character sheet has some Burden Slots numbered 1 - 20. Follow these instructions to add items to your burdens:

  1. Add 5 Rations of water to your Burdens (5 rations can stack in a single Burden Slot).
  2. If you chose a Mystic Gift in step 6, that gift occupies 1 Burden Slot.
  3. Roll on each of the Starting Gear Tables and add your starting gear to your Burdens. Weapons and armor will specify the amount of burden slots they occupy, while ammunition and other gear can "stack" multiples of itself in a single slot.
  4. Finally, roll on the Exotica table for a piece of Exotica. This occupies 1 burden slot.

7. Finishing Touches

Fill in these final details on your character sheet:

  • Level and XP: You are level 1, have 0 current XP and will require 10 XP to advance to level 2.
  • Hit Points (HP): To get your current and maximum Hit Points, roll 1d8. you may re-roll once, but must stick with the new result if you do so.
  • Maximum Burden Slots: Equal to your CON + 10.
    • You can indicate your maximum Burden Slots on your character sheet by putting an "X" next to the bottom-most slots. For example, if you have a CON of 1, you have 11 Maximum Burden Slots, and you will put X's next to slots 12 - 20 to signify that they can't be used.
    • If the items added to your burdens in step 7 exceed your maximum burden slots, you must choose items to get rid of until nothing occupies an "X'd" slot.
  • Readied Items: You can "Ready" a number of Burden Slots' worth of items up to your STR + 2.
    • You can indicate Readied slots by putting an "R" next to them. Readied items can be used reactively or in combat without using an action to retrieve them first.
    • Worn Items such as armor and clothing do not need to be readied to be used at will, and neither do Mystic Gifts. You can indicate a worn Burden Slot with a "W".

8. You're ready to play!

Send the referee a copy of your character sheet, or just tell him your Name, Ancestry, Personality, Background, Mystic Gift/Implant, and Armor Value, then use the Discord server to organize or join your first session.
