Character Creation

1. Print a Character Sheet and Read the Intro

  1. To get started, download and print a Blank Character Sheet. If it helps you, here's an example of a filled one.
  2. Read the 1-page setting intro, "What is Vaarn?", then return to these instructions.

2. Roll your Abilities

You have six core Abilities:

  • Strength (STR): melee attacks, physical power, athletic ability.
  • Dexterity (DEX): ranged attacks, speed, poise, finesse.
  • Constitution (CON): health recovery, carrying capacity, resistance to elements and intoxicants.
  • Psyche (PSY): ammo conservation, perception, judgement, intuition.
  • Intellect (INT): searching, general knowledge, language and tech skills (informed by your ancestry and background).
  • Ego (EGO): morale, willpower, persuasion, performance, charm, deception.

To determine your ability values, roll 3d6 (three six-sided dice) for each ability, in order. The lowest result of the three dice on each roll is the ability's value.

  • Example: You roll a 2, 2, and 6 for Strength. The lowest die is a 2, so your PC's Strength has a value of 2. Repeat this process for the rest of the abilities and record them on your Character Sheet.

3. Determine your Background

Use the generator below to roll a personality trait and background for your character and record them on your character sheet. Your background is meant to be a loose, but important starting point to help inform your character's history and perspective as you roleplay and explore within Vaarn. it will color how non-player characters might react to you, as well as you to them. It is up to you how and to what extent to interpret the results and significance of this roll, but the Referee is in your corner if you want to bounce ideas. Smart roleplay involves drawing on your background to gain the upper hand in applicable situations, but playing to your weaknesses can also enhance the experience.

4. Choose your Ancestry and Give your Character a Name

Choose an Ancestry from the table below. Your ancestry defines what type of life-form you are and what languages you know. Each Ancestry has a few rolling tables you may use to inspire your character’s name and appearance. Some of these tables have sparks for demeanour and the like, but the background generator we used in the last step makes them redundant. Only use them if you want your character to be more complex. Finally, each Ancestry also has one or two “Special” features. Be sure to add them to your Features list!

AncestryLanguages knownSpecial
True-Kin (human) Vaarnish, Hegemonic Standard Pure of Blood - You gain Advantage on reaction rolls when you encounter other true-kin. You lose this bonus if you ever become visibly mutated.
Inheritor - You have advantage on checks to identify and use pre-collapse technology.
Cacogen (mutant) Vaarnish Corrupted Blood - You must roll for 1-3 mutations during character creation.
Synth (robot or android) Vaarnish, LogLang syntax Synthetic Flesh - You do not need to eat or breathe and are immune to biological hazards, but you suffer double damage from electrical attacks.
Synthetic Mind - You are vulnerable to hacking and malicious LogLang code.
Newbeast (sapient animal) Vaarnish Beasthood - You gain advantage on checks wherever it would make sense for your animal nature to provide it, and disadvantage in circumstances where your animal nature would be unhelpful.
Mycomorph (corpse-fungus hybrid) Vaarnish Twice Born - You are formed from fungus and the corpse of a human. You can attempt to recall information that your original body knew. This may include information that has otherwise been lost during the Great Collapse.
Detritivor - You can consume organic matter in any state of decay and gain nourishment from it. Your healing from Short Rests is doubled if the meal you eat is rotting.

5. Record Derived Stats

Fill in these additional statistics on your character sheet:

  • Hit Points (HP): To get your current and maximum Hit Points, roll 1d8. you may re-roll once, but must stick with the new result if you do so.
  • Maximum Burden Slots: Equal to your CON + 10.

6. Choose a Talent or Cybernetic Implant

Pick 1 Talent or 1 Cybernetic Implant to add to your Features list.

  • You can roll here to see what the source of your talent might be. If you aren’t happy with the result you get, just re-roll it!
  • If you chose a Cybernetic Implant, be sure to update any relevant Ability values, and keep in mind that you may only ever have 1 Cybernetic implant installed per Ability.

7. Add items to your Burdens

  1. Roll on each of the Starting Gear Tables and add your starting gear to your Burdens. Weapons and armor will specify the amount of burden slots they occupy, while ammunition and other gear can "stack" multiples of itself in a single slot.
  2. Additionally, add 5 Rations of water to your Burdens (5 rations can stack in a single Burden Slot).
  3. Finally, roll on the Exotica table for a piece of Exotica.

8. You're ready to play!

Send the referee a copy of your character sheet, or just tell him your Name, Ancestry, Personality, Background, and Armor Value, then use the Discord server to organize or join your first session.
