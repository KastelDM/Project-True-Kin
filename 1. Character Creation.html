Character Creation

1. Print a Character Sheet and Read the Intro

  1. To get started, download and print a Blank Character Sheet. If it helps you, here's an example of a filled one.
  2. Read the 1-page setting intro, "What is Vaarn?"
  3. Ignore the section on the next page and carry on with these instructions, instead. This campaign uses a mixture of house rules and standard rules for Vaalts of Vaarn; so you may (but hopefully seldom) have to jump between these two resources:

    • KastelDM.github.io/Project-True-Kin is the domain where information specific to our campaign will be held; including house rules observed at our table. You may want to bookmark it!
    • vaarn.github.io, on the other hand, is a public resource containing the original rules authored by the creator of Vaults of Vaarn, Leo Hunt.

2. Roll your Abilities

Player Characters (PC's) have six core Abilities: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intellect, Psyche, and Ego.

  • Strength (STR): Used for melee attacks and tasks that require raw physical power or athletic ability.
  • Dexterity (DEX): Used for ranged attacks and tasks that require speed, poise, and finesse.
  • Constitution (CON): Used for health recovery, carrying capacity, and resistance to the elements.
  • Intellect (INT): Used for general knowledge as well as language and tech skills.
  • Psyche (PSY): Used for perception, judgment, and intuition.
  • Ego (EGO): Used for morale, willpower, persuasion, and deception.

Each ability has two related values: a Defense and a Bonus. When creating a PC, roll 3d6 (three six-sided dice) for each of their abilities, in order. The lowest of the three dice on each roll is the ability's bonus. Add 10 to find its defense.

  • Example: You roll a 2, 2, and 6 for Strength. The lowest die is a 2, so your PC's Strength has a bonus of +2 and a defense of 12. Repeat this process for the rest of the abilities and record them on your Character Sheet.

3. Choose your Ancestry and Give your Character a Name

Choose an Ancestry from the table below. Your ancestry determines what type of life-form you are and what languages you can speak. Each Ancestry has a few spark tables to give you inspiration for your character’s name, appearance, and personality. Each Ancestry also has a “Special” feature. Be sure to add it to your Features list!

4. Record Derived Stats

Fill in these additional statistics on your character sheet:

  • Hit Points (HP): To get your current and maximum Hit Points, roll 1d8. you may re-roll once, but must stick with the new result if you do so.
  • Inventory Slots: Equal to your CON bonus.
  • Energy Points (EP): The sum of your STR, DEX, and CON bonuses.
  • Focus Points (FP): The sum of your INT, PSY, and EGO bonuses.

5. Choose a Talent or Cybernetic Implant

Pick 1 Talent or 1 Cybernetic Implant to add to your Features list.

  • If you chose a Talent, be sure to record the type of talent that it is (Focus or Energy). You can roll here to see what the source of your talent might be. If you aren’t happy with the result you get, just re-roll it!
  • If you chose a Cybernetic Implant, be sure to update any relevant ability bonuses and defenses, and keep in mind that you may only ever have 1 Cybernetic implant installed per Ability.

6. Add items to your inventory

  1. Use the Starting Gear Table to roll and add your starting gear to your inventory.
  2. Additionally, add 5 Rations of water to your inventory (they can stack in a single Inventory Slot).
  3. Finally, roll on the Exotica table for a piece of Exotica.

7. You're ready to play!

Use the Discord server to organize or join your first session.
