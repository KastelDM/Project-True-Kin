Combat Rules

Surprise & Initiative

Combat proceeds in turns, alternating between the Player Characters (PCs) and the GM’s forces.

  • A surprised unit skips its first turn.
  • If neither side has clear advantage, roll a die:
    • Even result: PCs act first.
    • Odd result: GM acts first.

Combat Turns

On your side’s turn, each unit may take two actions, in any order or combination:

  • Move
  • Attack
  • Use an item or special feature
  • Interact with the environment
  • Improvise a stunt or other action
  • Prepare an action to trigger on a set condition (limit 1 prepared action)

In addition, a unit may speak or gesture briefly (about 6 seconds).

Movement

How the Move action works depends on the style of play:

  • Grid combat: Move up to 6 squares (unless noted otherwise). You can break your move up among your other actions (Example: Move 3 squares, attack, move 3 more squares).
  • Theatre of the Mind: Move to an adjacent area or into a new Range Band relative to your target.

Attacks

Attacks require two rolls: a To-Hit roll and a Damage roll.

  • To-Hit Roll: Roll a d20 and add the relevant ability bonus (usually STR for melee, DEX for ranged).
    • If the result equals or exceeds the target’s Armour, the attack hits.
    • A natural 20 is a critical hit: roll damage dice twice and total them.
    • A natural 1 always misses.
  • Damage Roll: On a hit, roll your weapon’s damage die. The target loses that many Hit Points.
  • Efficiency Tip: Roll your To-Hit and Damage dice together. Only apply damage if the attack hits.
  • Advantage & Disadvantage: Certain conditions grant Advantage (roll 2d20, keep the higher) or Disadvantage (roll 2d20, keep the lower).

Engagement & Attacks of Opportunity

  • If you are within the 'close' range of an enemy capable of attacking you, you are engaged with that enemy. Likewise, any enemies you are capable of striking are engaged by you.
  • While engaged, your ranged attacks are made with Disadvantage.
  • If an engaged enemy attempts to move out of your reach, you may make a free melee Attack of Opportunity. Resolve it as a normal Attack.

Range Bands

This game uses abstract Range Bands to represent distance. Ranged and Thrown weapons list their effective range, while Melee weapons generally have a range of 'close'. Ranged attacks made against targets outside a weapon’s range band are rolled with Disadvantage.

  • Close: Within reach; melee distance. Typically 1 square (2 with 'reach' weapons).
  • Short: Shotgun range. 2–6 squares.
  • Mid: Pistol or Rifle range. About 7–20 squares.
  • Long: Sniper range. About 21–60 squares.
  • Distant: Barely visible. 61+ squares.
