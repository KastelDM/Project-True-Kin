Talents

d20 Talent Talent Type Description
1 Brawler Energy When you hit with any improvised or unarmed attack, it will always deal at least an amount of damage equal to your level.
2 Bullet Bender Energy or Focus You can make ranged attacks against targets all the way up to Long range without incurring disadvantage on the To-hit roll. (In other words, you ignore the "optimal range" of ranged weaponry.)
3 Bullet Sponge Energy When you take kinetic damage, the amount of the damage is reduced by 1 for each damage die rolled against you.
4 Commander Focus The last target you attack on your turn is marked. The next ally to attack your marked target before the start of your next turn gains advantage on the To-hit roll.
5 Cryokinetic Focus You can spend Hit Points to generate intense cold.
6 Defender Energy You can swap places with Allies within Close range when they are subject to an attack.
7 Hacker Focus You can spend Hit Points to gain a bonus to checks to interface with computer systems or non-sentient robots.
8 Persistent Distraction Energy When you attack a creature, It has disadvantage on the next attack it makes against a target that isn't you before the start of your next turn.
9 Persistent Threat Energy You can make up to 3 Attacks of Opportunity on each round of combat.
10 Precognate Focus As an action, you can spend d8 Hit Points to roll a d20, known as a Precognition Die, and record the result. You can then use this result in place of one ability check made by yourself or a creature you can see. Unused Precognition Die vanish after a long rest.
  
  You can also spend Hit Points to consult your oracular abilities and gain a sense of what might happen in the future.
11 Projectionist Focus While you are unconscious or maintaining a meditative trance, you can project your consciousness outside of your body by spending Hit Points. While projecting, you can perceive everything in the physical world as normal, but you cannot interact with it, and only psychic creatures or devices have a chance of detecting you.
12 Pyrokinetic Focus You can spend Hit Points to generate intense heat or flames.
13 Seer Focus You are sensitive to lingering memories and past events. You can spend Hit Points to gain insight into the history of a person or place that you can see.
14 Shade Focus You can spend Hit Points to turn invisible.
15 Swashbuckler Energy You can move up to 1 extra square when moving on your turn, and you are immune to Attacks of Opportunity from creatures you attacked previously in the same turn.
16 Tactician Focus When you use the Move action, you can move an ally instead of yourself.
17 Telekinetic Focus You can spend Hit Points to move creatures or objects with your mind.
18 Telepath Focus You can communicate telepathically with biological creatures and read their surface thoughts. This might cost Hit Points depending on circumstance.
19 Tough Energy You gain +2 Maximum Hit Points per level, and also gain +2 Maximum Hit Points whenever you level up.
20 Vibrokinetic Energy or Focus You can spend Hit Points to generate vibrations at different frequencies and amplitudes.
